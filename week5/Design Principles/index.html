Design Principles

Signal to Noise Ratio

Good Example:Google

Googles main page has a wonderful signal to noise ratio; it only shows exactly what you need

Bad Example:Facebook

Facebook has way too many ads and suggested posts, making it difficult to find material that im actually interested in.

Hicks Law

Good Example:Youtube-mp3

Simple menu, just put in a youtube link and the download starts!

Bad Example:MSU Schedule of Courses

So many options are shown, and it makes it hard to determine what fields you actually have to fill out to see classes

Chunking

Good Example:Reddit

Information is chunked into individual posts, making it easy to find the post or information that you are looking for, and avoiding information you dont want

Bad Example:PHP manual

Too much information on one function is chunked in to one page.

Affordance

Good Example:Amazon

Buttons are shaped like arrows, making it obvious that their function is to change to the next picture. hovering over a product comes up with a bigger zoomed in picure. Everything does what you would expect it to and buttons are color coded, darker colors being more important buttons

Bad Example:Download.com

When on a page of a program, ads will display that say "download" or other similar phrases that confuse you and make it hard to find the actual download link

Mapping

Good Example:Google Drive

Buttons do what they should: select box allows deleting or moving files, dragging files can move them to other folders, and dragging files onto the website stores them in the current open folder

Bad Example:Amazon

Amazon has a 1-click order button, which is displayed right below "add to cart". Users could easily place an order on something they dont want with 1 simple mis-clic
